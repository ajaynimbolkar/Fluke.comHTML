History
 

Our company was founded by John Fluke in Springdale, Connecticut, USA in 1948. From the very beginning it  specialized in electrical test and measurement products. Early in its history, Fluke recognized that the very best electrical metrology would be needed to support its products and undertook to develop this capability. Out of this activity, the calibration business was born. Over the years many breakthrough calibration products were introduced, establishing Fluke as a leader in electrical metrology; examples are the 301 Series Precision Power Supplies launched in 1952 and the first Multifunction Calibrator (the 706), which was brought to the market in 1967.

In 2000 Fluke acquired Wavetek Precision Measurement, followed a year later by the acquisition of Hart Scientific – this extended Fluke’s calibration business into the temperature field. Further expansion came in the business of pressure and gas flow calibration with the acquisition of DH Instruments (2007), and then Ruska and Pressurements (2010).

Fluke Calibration brings together the original Fluke electrical calibration business and all these acquisitions in one unified, worldwide organization. This broad calibration business has unmatched breadth and depth in metrology, putting Fluke in a unique position to deliver today’s and tomorrow’s calibration solutions. It’s a promise you can count on for the long term.
